In 2015, the Paris Climate Accord brought together nations from all around the globe to resolve a long neglected issue. Today, the international Climathon extends the reach of climate action to thousands of individuals in 59 cities. At every local hackathon, participants will have 24 hours to resolve a problem set by their local organizers. Much like other cities, the Toronto cohort (organized my MaxQ) set a local goal to reduce the TTC's carbon emissions by 5%. Operating a cost neutral budget, my team and I tackled this challenge by determining the most polluting bus routes.
