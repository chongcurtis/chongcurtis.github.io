In recent decades, threats such as overfishing, habitat destruction, and pollution have placed global fish populations in peril. To involve public opinion, US Department of State organized fishackathon, a three-day hackathon that challenges the public to tackle industry issues. Alongside 43 other cities, Toronto hosted the event and invited the president of Coke and the Water Brothers to inspire the 20 teams that attended. With an assortment of challenges ranging from fish identification to the retrieval of diving equptment, our team chose to create an algorithm to locate the source of Asian Carp breeding areas.
