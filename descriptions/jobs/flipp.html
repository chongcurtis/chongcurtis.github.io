Flipp was an absolute dream-come-true for 17-year-old me.

As an intern, I built a resume classifier to predict if a candidate would receive an interview.

Since our existing applicant tracking system accepted resumes in all formats, I built a resume parser that categorized sections of a resume by tracking the position of each header. With the data normalized, I ran the resumes through traditional NLP techniques (stemming, n-grams, lemmatization, etc.). Lastly, I created many bags of words and TF-IDF embeddings for my final model, an SVM ensemble.

Presenting my data explorations with weekly graphs was a highlight of my internship. I also loved trying their HTC Vive while waiting for my models to train.

I definitely couldn't have gotten through this internship without the help of Ashwin Tumne, I still constantly think about your advice on using overlapping distibutions.
