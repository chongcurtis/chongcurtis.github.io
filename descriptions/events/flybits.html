My team Marcel O’Neil, Kevin Shen, and Daniel Wu met for the first time at the “Flybits Context Camp”, a three-day hackathon at Lighthouse Labs. Flybits is a service that collects environmental data from a user’s phone to provide developers with contextual information. The company then proccesses this data to deliver information ranging from the number of steps taken to the gender of a user. Using their api, our team created MinoTour, a tourism app that recommends nearby tourist attractions based on user context. At the end, my team headed home with the People’s Choice awards and the promise of more memories.
