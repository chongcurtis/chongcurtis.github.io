DabCoin is a Proof-of-dab based cryptocurrency. It runs on a proof-of-work algorithm that only mines blocks if the data from your device accelerometer indicates that you are dabbing.

At the hackathon, Marcel and David hacked away at the React-based front end web wallet, the cryptocurrency network nodes, and the API for clients to interact with the network. Since I was in charge of the dab-or-not-dab classifier, I hosted a site on Nginx to collect training data from my phone’s accelerometer and gyroscope.

With a hundred training examples spanning over a 5-second dab (and not dab), these frames were then saved into a data frame for a Keras model to ingest. For every recorded dab, I stacked each measurement one after another in a successive line. The rationale behind this was so that each 1D convolution layer would recognize how a dab would alter these measurements over time.

Excitedly, over the course of 200 epochs, its loss shrunk to 1.3157e-06 and the model resulted with an f-score of 0.954.

Looking back, I think the primary reason for the model’s success was a combination of well-varied testing data and the extreme accuracy of my iphone8’s accelerometer and gyroscope. Great job Apple!

All that’s left is to package up the model and write a Flash script to connect it to the network.
