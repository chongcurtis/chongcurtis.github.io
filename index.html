Hello!

Welcome to my corner of the internet. I'm a programmer with the ambition of becoming the next CEO, pioneering another digital revolution (Bill Gates anyone?). As a high school student, I try to squeeze enough time for hackathons, hiking trips, and the occasional ML Book Club. I also love working with data and aspire to influence policy in the city I love. So without further adieu, here lies an archive of my past projects, work experiences, and achievements. Mind if you scroll down?

Work Experience

As Canada's first space accelerator, MaxQ aims to lead space startups to success by providing mentorship and capital to emerging start-ups. At my time as an intern at the accelerator, I assisted one of their companies Skywatch by creating an API that parses HDF files (from NASA's satellites) into JSON. This data is then inserted into a Postgres database where third parties can query the API by geological region and time.


Toronto Hacker Club


Toronto Hacker club is an organization that strives towards promoting Computer Science education towards students. The club has hosted two hackathons to date, an introduction to web design workshop with Mozilla, and have been awarded the "Building Community through Sciences and Technology Award" by the Toronto Youth Cabinet. As the Director of External Affairs at Toronto Hacker Club, I find myself running across town and meeting professionals throughout the city. I also assist participants at our events with technical issues they may face.

STEM Fellowship


After placing first at the STEM Fellowship's Big Data Challenge, I jumped down a rabbit hole and joined the Fellowship's Data Science Team to advocate for data science education. The nonprofit is run by students at Canadian universities who hope to equip fellow colleagues with skills in data science and scholarly writing. At the organization, I pass my days by preparing datasets for our Kaggle-like competitions and organize academic reporting challenges.

The Fountain of Love and Life


In the ninth grade, I interned at The Fountain of Love and Life, an organization that produces film and music for an audience of more than 1 million at the Ontario Fairchild television station. During my time with the organization, I designed and developed a PHP plugin to transform excel files into WordPress posts, which facilitated the company's ability to make announcements. I was also entrusted with the task of developing Edmond Lo's personal website, a company client.
