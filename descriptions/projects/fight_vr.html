FightVR was my first official foray into vr gear and a revisit into making games. Coming from a diverse background, my team and I wanted to control a sword and shield and battle like gladiators in a VR arena.

Using a Myo armband, the accelerometer from a phone, and a Google cardboard, our game would take user input to reflect changes in a VR shield and sword, allowing blocking and attacking mechanics to feel very natural.

To begin, another teammate and I started to work on a C# Unity server to communicate with all these modules and sync it to the game. After firewall issues and hours debugging Unity’s API, we added enemy units and damage effects into the game world. Our favourite addition was a teleport button on the android app because it eliminated nauseous navigation around the arena.

Overall we won the best VR/AR hack at AdrenaLAN and I have thank my teammates Ethan Guo, Mufeez Amjad, Leon Fattakhov, and Advait Maybhate for a fantastic time.
