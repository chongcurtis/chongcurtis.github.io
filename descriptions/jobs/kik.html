The theme at Kik's internship was internal tools

At Kik, I rebuilt their A/B testing framework to allow for fine-grained demographic segmentation. On top of the A/B testing, I developed was a script to sequentially deploy Airflow tasks in the proper order; reducing job failures when data integrity was poor. Aside from frameworks, I pushed many other changes to their spam infrastructure to monitor the relevance of ML variables and identify uncaught spam accounts.

Kik kicked me in the side and said: “Hey, internal tools are fun to make”. That was a side of software development I thought little of. Altogether, I had a lot of fun staying late competing in Kaggle competitions, writing notes on all things Dale Carnegie, and working on some side projects.

It was the best vacation from school I could’ve asked for.
