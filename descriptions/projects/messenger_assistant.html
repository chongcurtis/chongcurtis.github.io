I think communication is a field that tech hasn't properly explored yet. Chatbots have failed, and even Messenger is moving back to the basics (which is not a bad thing).

What surprises me is that we spend a significant amount of time talking to people online, but the services around that conversation are primitive, nothing like the suite of signals that other industries such as navigation have implemented.

Here are some ideas I have to improve conversations:
  • A chat extension that helps you speak better by showing how your sentence can be rephrased.
  • A bot that reads the conversation and pulls up talking points. Let's be honest, some conversations are quite dry.
My hope is for Messenger Assistant to provide the API that makes these tools available. So far, you can:
  • Put down reminders to talk to someone.
  • Copy and Paste emojis
  • See the character count in your conversation over time.
Messenger Assistant is a chrome extension that provides you with information about your conversation. My hope is for it to be a starting point for the moonshot ideas I mentioned above.
